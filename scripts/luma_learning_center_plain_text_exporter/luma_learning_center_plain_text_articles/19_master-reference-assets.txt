Master Reference Assets =======================

Before you start generating variants, refining outputs, or scaling creative directions in Luma, establish your Master Reference Assets .

Your Master Reference Assets are the immovable elements of your project — the non-negotiables that define identity and consistency. Think of them as the Agent's single source of truth .

These can include:

- A logo and its placement rules

- A brand color system

- A character’s face, proportions, and wardrobe

- A product’s exact physical design

- A specific lighting style

- A defined tone of voice

- A tagline that must remain unchanged

Luma is designed for exploration and scale. You can generate dozens of variations from a single prompt. You can branch into new formats. You can transform images into video, add voiceover, localize, restyle, extend, and reframe.

but…

Iteration without anchoring tends to drift. -------------------------------------------

When you tell the Agent to fix this version or generate another option , it naturally optimizes from the most recent output. If you don’t explicitly remind it to reference the original Master Reference Assets , small deviations can compound:

fix this version

generate another option

- A character’s facial structure shifts slightly.

- A logo subtly changes proportions.

- A brand color drifts warmer or cooler.

- A product’s shape becomes approximate.

- A tagline mutates over iterations.

At first, the changes are subtle. Over time, they become inconsistencies. Master Reference Assets prevent creative entropy. They ensure that while exploration expands outward, identity remains stable. In a system built for scale, anchoring is discipline.

Ask for edits with context --------------------------

Luma allows you to iterate quickly and generate many variations from a single input. But the more you scale, the more intentional you must be in your instructions about what stays constant.

When refining a version, don’t just reference the last output – re-anchor the Agent to your original Master Reference Assets .

Instead of:

Fix the lighting on this.

Say:

Fix the lighting while preserving the exact character proportions, logo placement, and color palette from the Master Reference Assets.

That small shift in instruction protects the integrity of your project across every transformation — image to video, video to localization, edit to extension.

Consistency doesn’t happen automatically. It’s maintained deliberately.

Key takeaway ------------

Before you scale, define your Master Reference Assets . As you iterate, always re-anchor to them. In Luma, creativity can expand infinitely — but identity should remain intentional and grounded in a clear single source of truth .
